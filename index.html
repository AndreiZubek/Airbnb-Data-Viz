Data Visualization Project Group 70

An Analysis of Airbnb Pricing in New York City

This project studies how location and room type shape Airbnb prices across NYC. The goal is to identify pricing patterns and reveal how the short term rental market varies by borough.

Introduction

Analyzing Airbnb data in NYC provides insight into how price, neighborhood, location, and room type shape the city’s short term housing market. NYC has boroughs and neighborhoods with very different demand patterns, so listing prices vary significantly across space and property type. This analysis highlights how tourism activity, neighborhood popularity, and listing characteristics influence market outcomes.

This topic is important because Airbnb has transformed urban tourism and local housing markets. Understanding pricing patterns helps hosts choose better listing strategies, helps travelers compare options, and gives policymakers evidence to evaluate housing and rental impacts in local communities.

Introduction to the Data

Dataset used: Kaggle Link

Data size: original dataset has 48,895 rows. After cleaning the data, 5,000 rows are used for our visualizations.

Main attributes: id, name, host_id, host_name, neighbourhood_group, neighbourhood, latitude, longitude, room_type, price, minimum_nights, number_of_reviews, last_review, reviews_per_month, calculated_host_listings_count, availability_365.

Room types: Entire home/apt, Private room, Shared room.
Boroughs: Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, Bronx, Staten

Visualization 1: Average Price by Borough

Interaction instructions: Use the dropdown inside the chart to switch between All, Entire home/apt, Private room, and Shared room. Hover on bars to see exact average price values.
