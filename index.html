An Analysis of Airbnb Pricing in New York City

This project explores the pricing landscape of Airbnb listings across New York City's five boroughs using a cleaned sample of 5,000 listings from 2019. Through five interactive visualizations, we examine how location, room type, neighborhood, and reviews together shape what hosts charge and what travelers pay. Our goal is to reveal both broad market patterns and the finer details that shape pricing across the city.

Introduction

Airbnb prices in New York City are not spread evenly across the map. A listing in Manhattan does not compete in the same market as one in the Bronx, and an entire apartment is fundamentally priced differently from a private or shared room. These differences are not random, but rather reflect deeper patterns in how the city is structured, how tourists move through it, and how hosts respond to local demand.

For travelers, these differences matter because price alone does not tell the full story of a listing's value. Two listings with similar nightly rates may offer very different experiences depending on location, room type, and access to transportation or attractions. Understanding these patterns helps travelers make more informed choices and avoid overpaying for convenience or location without realizing it.

Introduction to the Data

Dataset used: Kaggle Link

Data size: The original dataset includes 48,895 listings. For this analysis, we used a cleaned and filtered sample of 5,000 listings from 2019 to ensure consistency and reduce noise from extreme outliers or incomplete entries.

Main attributes:Each listing includes key attributes that help explain how travelers evaluate Airbnb options, including location (borough, neighborhood, latitude, and longitude), listing characteristics (room type and minimum stay requirements), and pricing information. The dataset also includes engagement indicators such as number of reviews, review frequency, host activity, and availability throughout the year.

Room types: Entire home/apt, Private room, Shared room.
Boroughs: Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, Bronx, Staten Island

Visualization 1: Geographic Price Distribution

Interaction instructions: Hover over a point to see the listing name, price, room type, and review count. The colors show price categories from budget to luxury. Use the dropdown menu to explore listings by borough.

Takeaway: This visualization maps Airbnb listings across New York City using their geographic coordinates (latitude and longitude), with each point representing a listing. The color of each point represents price, ranging from lower-priced listings (yellow) to higher-priced listings (red). We see a strong concentration of listings in Manhattan and parts of Brooklyn, with far fewer listings in the outer boroughs. The darkest reds, which represent the highest prices, are heavily clustered in central Manhattan, especially in dense urban areas. Brooklyn shows a mix of mid-to-high price listings, particularly in neighborhoods closer to Manhattan. Queens, the Bronx, and Staten Island show fewer listings overall and mostly lighter colors, indicating lower prices. This visualization clearly shows that Airbnb pricing in New York City is highly location-dependent. Manhattan dominates both in density and price level, while Brooklyn serves as a secondary high-demand market. On the other hand, the outer boroughs are significantly more affordable and less densely listed.

Visualization 2: Price Distribution by Room Type

Interaction instructions: Hover over each room type to see the quartiles, median price, and listing count. The legend above the chart explains how to read the box plot.

Takeaway: This visualization compares Airbnb prices across three room types in New York City: entire homes/apartments, private rooms, and shared rooms. It uses a box plot to show the distribution of prices, including the median, spread (quartiles), and variability within each category. Entire homes/apartments are clearly the most expensive option, with the highest median price and the widest range of values, stretching from relatively affordable listings to very high-end stays. Private rooms sit in the middle, with a lower median price and a tighter spread compared to entire homes. Shared rooms are the most affordable overall, with the lowest median price and the smallest overall range, indicating more consistent pricing in that category. Room type strongly structures the Airbnb pricing market in NYC. Entire homes function as a premium, highly variable product, while private rooms offer a mid-range balance of cost and privacy. Shared rooms represent the lowest-cost, most standardized option for travelers.

Visualization 3: Average Price by Neighborhood

Interaction instructions: Hover over a bar to see the neighborhood, borough, listing count, and average price. The bars are sorted by price, and the colors separate the boroughs.

Takeaway: This visualization highlights the average nightly price for Airbnb listings across New York City’s most expensive neighborhoods. It uses a color-coded bar chart to compare costs across the five boroughs, allowing travelers to identify which specific areas command a premium. Tottenville (Staten Island) and Bayside (Queens) emerge as the most expensive neighborhoods on average, with prices reaching the $300 mark. While Manhattan (teal) dominates the list with the highest number of premium-priced neighborhoods, its individual averages like Greenwich Village ($192) or the Financial District ($170) are actually lower than the top outliers in other boroughs. Brooklyn and the Bronx also make strong appearances, with areas like DUMBO and Riverdale offering high-end stays that rival Manhattan's pricing. The takeaway for travelers is that premium pricing is not exclusive to Manhattan. While Manhattan offers the most "luxury" neighborhoods in total, the most expensive nightly rates are currently found in specific pockets of Staten Island and Queens. For those seeking a balance of popularity and price, neighborhoods like Greenwich Village provide a mid-to-high range option with a significant number of available listings.

Visualization 4: Pricing Patterns in Guest Engagement

Interaction instructions: Left chart (density heatmap): Hover to see clusters of listings by price and review count. Darker = more listings. Right chart (bars): Hover to see average and median reviews for each price range.

Takeaway:This visualization explores the relationship between Airbnb pricing and guest reviews in New York City. Using a density heatmap and a bar chart, it illustrates how often listings at different price points are actually booked and reviewed. The density heatmap (left) reveals that the vast majority of listings cluster at the lower end of the review scale (0–30 reviews), regardless of price. However, the bar chart (right) shows a clear trend: as the price increases, the average number of reviews decreases. Listings in the $0–$100 range boast the highest average review count (roughly 70 reviews), while premium listings priced over $350 see significantly less review activity. Pricing and popularity are inversely linked in the NYC market. For travelers, this suggests that budget-friendly options are the most frequently cycled and tested by the community. While high-end listings offer luxury, they are booked less often, meaning they accumulate the "social proof" of guest reviews much more slowly than their budget counterparts.

Visualization 5: Borough and Room Type Analysis

Interaction instructions: Use the dropdown above the chart to switch between average price and average availability views. Hover over a cell to see the borough, room type, listing count, and the selected metric value. The color scale next to the chart summarizes the pattern.

Takeaway: This duo of heatmaps illustrates the relationship between cost and availability for Airbnb listings across New York City’s boroughs. By comparing these two metrics, travelers can see where the highest demand meets the highest prices. The Price Heatmap confirms that Manhattan and entire homes/apartments command the highest rates citywide, while shared rooms consistently offer the lowest barrier to entry. However, the Availability Heatmap reveals a critical trade-off: many of those high-cost areas, particularly in Manhattan and Brooklyn, show significantly lower availability (indicated by the lighter yellow/green shades). This suggests that despite the premium price, demand for entire homes in central locations remains high, leaving fewer dates open for travelers. The takeaway for travelers is a "price vs. planning" balance. If you are looking for an entire home in Manhattan or Brooklyn, you’ll likely need to book much further in advance due to low availability and high costs. Conversely, if you're planning a last-minute trip, private rooms or listings in The Bronx and Staten Island offer more consistent year-round availability and a more budget-friendly price point.

Conclusion

Across these five visualizations, a clear narrative emerges: location and room type are the primary architects of the Airbnb market in New York City. Manhattan consistently sets the price ceiling across all categories, and opting for an entire home remains the most significant cost driver for travelers, regardless of which borough they call home. However, the data reveals deeper nuances that avid travelers should note. Neighborhood-level analysis shows that "hidden gems" in the outer boroughs, driven by local attractions and transit access, can rival Manhattan prices. Furthermore, the inverse relationship between price and review volume suggests that the city’s most active community isn’t found in luxury stays, but in the budget sector, where high booking turnover creates a robust landscape of peer-reviewed options. Looking ahead, while this 2019 data gives us a great starting point, the NYC rental market has changed a lot since then. Future studies should look at how the pandemic and the rise of remote work have shifted prices and where people choose to stay. New, stricter city laws have also limited the number of available Airbnbs, which is a huge factor to consider. Adding local details like neighborhood demographics could also help explain why some blocks are so much pricier than others. Ultimately, while Manhattan is the go-to for luxury, the real heart of the NYC market is in the affordable, highly-rated listings that travelers rely on across all five boroughs.
